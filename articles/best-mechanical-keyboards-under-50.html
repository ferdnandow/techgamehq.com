Best Mechanical Keyboards Under $50 in 2026

By TechGameHQ Staff May 2026 6 min read Review

Mechanical keyboards used to cost a fortune. In 2026, you can get real mechanical switches, solid build quality, and RGB lighting for under $50. These picks prove you don't need to spend $150 to type and game on a proper mechanical board.

Switch Types — What to Choose

Before picking a keyboard, know your switches:

  • Red switches — Linear, light, fast. Best for gaming. No tactile bump, very quiet.
  • Brown switches — Tactile bump without a click. Best all-rounder for gaming and typing.
  • Blue switches — Tactile + audible click. Great feedback for typing, loud for gaming. Bad for shared spaces.
💡 First Mechanical Keyboard? Go with Red or Brown switches. Red for pure gaming, Brown if you also type a lot. Blue switches sound satisfying but your teammates on voice chat will hate you.

2. Royal Kludge RK61 ~$45

The RK61 is a 60% wireless mechanical keyboard at an incredible price. Bluetooth 5.0, USB-C connection, and a 1800mAh battery that lasts weeks between charges. The 60% layout is polarizing — no arrow keys or function row — but it leaves your desk clean and your mouse arm more comfortable. If you can adapt to the layout, this is exceptional value.

The Verdict

For most gamers getting their first mechanical keyboard, the Redragon K552 is the easy pick — solid, affordable, and widely available. If you want wireless and don't mind learning a 60% layout, the RK61 is exceptional for the money. And if you want to start building a taste for enthusiast keyboards without a big investment, the Epomaker TH80 Pro is worth every cent at $50.

Once you go mechanical, you won't go back to membrane. The difference in feel — both for gaming and typing — is immediately obvious.
