Top 10 Indie Games Worth Your Time in 2026

By Jessielle Patrícia May 2026 7 min read Gaming

Indie games have never been better. Small studios are making experiences that rival — and often surpass — big budget titles. These 10 games are worth every minute you put into them.

1. Hades II ~$30
Roguelike / Action

The sequel to one of the greatest indie games ever made. Hades II already has more content than most finished games. The combat is fluid, the writing is brilliant, and the replay value is near infinite.

Why play it: Best roguelike ever made, runs on low-end PCs, hundreds of hours of content.
2. Hollow Knight ~$15
Metroidvania

A dark, beautiful underground world with tight platforming and challenging combat. Hollow Knight offers 40+ hours of content at a fraction of AAA prices. One of the most atmospheric games ever created.

Why play it: Masterpiece level art and music, enormous world, deeply satisfying combat.
3. Stardew Valley ~$15
Farming / RPG

Made by a single developer, Stardew Valley has sold over 30 million copies. It's cozy, deep, and endlessly charming. Build your farm, make friends, explore mines, fall in love. Runs on literally anything.

Why play it: Perfect game to unwind with. Runs on any PC. Endless content with regular free updates.
4. Balatro ~$15
Roguelike / Card Game

Balatro is a poker-inspired roguelike that's impossibly addictive. It won multiple Game of the Year awards and deservedly so. Easy to learn, incredibly deep.

Why play it: One more run syndrome at its finest. Runs on any PC. Genuinely innovative gameplay.
5. Celeste ~$20
Platformer

A precision platformer with heart. Celeste is challenging but fair, with an accessibility system that lets anyone enjoy it. The story deals with anxiety and mental health in a genuinely moving way.

Why play it: Best platformer of the decade. Meaningful story. Runs on any PC.
6. Disco Elysium ~$20
RPG / Detective

A detective RPG unlike anything else. No combat — just dialogue, choices, and one of the most unique skill systems in gaming. The writing is some of the best in any medium. Essential for RPG fans.

Why play it: Unmatched writing, completely original concept, runs on any PC.
7. Vampire Survivors ~$5
Survivor / Roguelite

For $5, Vampire Survivors offers hundreds of hours of gameplay. You survive waves of enemies, collect power-ups, and watch chaos unfold. Incredibly addictive and runs on anything — even a toaster.

Why play it: Best value game ever made. Runs on anything. Perfect for short sessions.
8. Terraria ~$10
Sandbox / Adventure

Often called the best game ever made per dollar. Terraria has been updated for over 10 years with no additional cost. Build, explore, fight bosses, and craft in a procedurally generated world with infinite replay value.

Why play it: Infinite content, fantastic multiplayer, runs on any PC.
9. Dave the Diver ~$20
Adventure / Management

Dive during the day, run a sushi restaurant at night. Dave the Diver is charming, funny, and surprisingly deep. One of the most original games in years with a unique gameplay loop that never gets old.

Why play it: Completely unique experience, great humor, perfect pacing.
10. Undertale ~$10
RPG

A classic that holds up perfectly in 2026. Undertale subverts RPG conventions in ways that are still surprising. Multiple endings, unforgettable characters, and a soundtrack that fans still listen to years later.

Why play it: Genuinely surprising story, runs on anything, essential gaming experience.

Why These Indie Games Stand Out in 2026

The indie game market has exploded over the past decade, and with it comes a filtering problem: there are now hundreds of thousands of indie games on Steam, and the vast majority of them aren't worth your time. The 10 games on this list have been selected based on a simple criterion — they're games that have genuinely stuck with players years after release, generating the kind of word-of-mouth and community enthusiasm that no marketing budget can buy.

What unites them is craft. Whether it's the pixel-perfect combat of Hollow Knight, the deceptively simple but endlessly deep card mechanics of Balatro, or the extraordinary writing of Disco Elysium, each game does something exceptionally well that many AAA titles fail to achieve. And they do it at a fraction of the cost — most of these games sell for $10-30 and regularly go on deep sale.

It's also worth noting how well these games run on modest hardware. Every game on this list will run on a PC that would struggle with modern AAA releases. If you're gaming on a budget system, these titles represent incredible value — high-quality experiences without the hardware investment. What Makes a Great Indie Game in 2026

The definition of "indie" has shifted considerably. Some of the games on this list — Hades II, Disco Elysium — had significant budgets and teams. What defines them as indie is creative independence: no publisher dictating mechanics to maximize monetization, no focus group testing the storyline into blandness. The creative vision comes through unfiltered, which is why indie games so often feel more alive than their AAA counterparts.

The games that last are the ones that respect player time and intelligence. Balatro trusts you to discover its depth rather than tutorializing every mechanic. Celeste's assist mode gives you complete control over difficulty without judging you for using it. Undertale lets your choices have genuine weight. These are design philosophies that are rare in big-budget games and common in the best indie titles.

If you're building a complete gaming setup to play these on, the good news is you don't need expensive hardware. A setup centered around a decent budget monitor can make even pixel art games look great. Pair that with good audio and you're set. Even the best gaming headsets under $50 deliver excellent stereo sound that brings games like Hades II and Hollow Knight to life.

Tips to Get the Most Out of Indie Games

Go in blind when possible. The best indie games are designed to surprise you. Undertale, Disco Elysium, and Hollow Knight all have moments that hit much harder if you don't know they're coming. Resist looking up guides until you're stuck — the discovery process is part of the experience.

Try before you buy during Steam Next Fest. Steam runs "Next Fest" events twice a year where thousands of games offer free demos. This is the best opportunity to try indie games before committing — many studios put their strongest content in demos specifically to hook new players.

Explore the game's community after finishing. Games like Undertale and Hollow Knight have rich communities that add enormous value post-completion — fan theories, alternative runs, challenge content, and fan art that deepen your appreciation of what you just played. The Reddit communities for these games are among the most welcoming in gaming.

Record your first playthroughs. Many of the best moments in games like Balatro and Hades II happen spontaneously and are worth having on record. It takes very little system resources to capture these games — our guide on how to record your gameplay for free explains how to set up OBS for low-impact recording that runs alongside even older hardware.

Frequently Asked Questions

Are indie games generally shorter than AAA games?

Not necessarily — and the games on this list prove it. Stardew Valley, Terraria, and Hades II can provide hundreds of hours of content. Hollow Knight offers 40+ hours for a first playthrough with substantial optional content beyond that. The main story of Disco Elysium is 15-25 hours depending on how thoroughly you explore. Where indie games are sometimes shorter is in cinematic presentation — cutscenes and voice acting are resource-intensive, so smaller studios often invest that budget in gameplay depth instead.

Where should I buy indie games to best support developers?

Steam is the most convenient platform and the developers get roughly 70% of the sale price. itch.io lets developers set their own cut (sometimes 100%), which makes it the most developer-supportive option for smaller releases. GOG offers DRM-free downloads if that matters to you. For the games on this list, Steam is fine — the developers are established enough that a Steam sale does well by them, and the Steam ecosystem (cloud saves, achievements, community features) adds genuine value.

Do I need a gaming PC to play these indie games?

No — that's one of the great strengths of indie games. Most of the titles on this list run comfortably on a laptop with integrated graphics. Vampire Survivors ($5) runs on practically anything with a screen. Stardew Valley lists Intel HD 4000 as its minimum GPU. Even Hades II runs on fairly modest hardware. If you're gaming on a budget and want to know exactly what hardware to prioritize, our guide on how to choose PC parts on a budget explains where to spend and where to save.

Where to Start

If you've never played indie games seriously, start with Vampire Survivors ($5) or Stardew Valley ($15) — both are accessible, low-spec, and will give you hundreds of hours of entertainment. If you want a challenge, go straight to Hollow Knight or Hades II.

All of these are available on Steam and frequently go on sale for 50-80% off during seasonal sales.
