What to Look for in a Gaming Chair Under $150

At this price, you’re making trade-offs. Here’s what to prioritize:

  • Lumbar support — Either a built-in lumbar curve or a detachable pillow. Non-negotiable if you sit for long sessions.
  • Adjustable armrests — At minimum, height-adjustable. 4D armrests (up/down, left/right, forward/back, pivot) are usually only on $200+ chairs.
  • Recline range — Look for at least 135° recline with a lockable position. Useful for taking breaks without leaving the chair.
  • Weight capacity — Most chairs in this range support 250–330 lbs. Check if you’re near the upper limit — material quality degrades faster under constant heavy load.
  • Foam density — Budget chairs often use low-density foam that flattens within a year. Dowinx and RESPAWN hold up better than most here.
💡 Foam vs Mesh at This Price Mesh chairs (like office chairs) are better for hot climates and long sessions — they breathe better than PU leather foam chairs. However, true ergonomic mesh chairs (Herman Miller, Steelcase) start at $400+. The cheap mesh chairs under $150 have poor lumbar curves and short mesh lifespans. At this budget, foam/PU leather gaming chairs offer better structured support.

Gaming Chair vs Office Chair: Which Should You Buy?

This is a real debate. A $150 office chair from a brand like HON or IKEA (MARKUS) will often provide better lumbar support and durability than a $150 gaming chair. Gaming chairs look great and have a higher recline — but office chairs win on ergonomics for the same money.

Choose a gaming chair if: you want the aesthetic, you recline frequently, or you want a footrest option.

Choose an office chair if: you sit upright for work and gaming and prioritize long-term back health.

The Verdict

For pure value under $150, the Dowinx LS-6658D is our top pick — the footrest and massage lumbar alone justify the price over competitors. If you sit for long work+gaming sessions, the RESPAWN 110 edges it out on structured ergonomic support. Under $100, the Homall S-Racer is a fine starting point before you can upgrade.

None of these will last 10 years. But at this price, you’re getting real comfort improvements over a dining chair or cheap desk chair — and your back will thank you after a 4-hour session.
