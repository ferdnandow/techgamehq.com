Best Budget Graphics Cards in 2026

The GPU market in 2026 is better for budget buyers than it’s been in years. Strong new mid-range cards, falling used prices on last-gen hardware, and genuine 1080p performance under $300 — here’s exactly where to spend your money.

Our Top Picks

🏆 Best New GPU Under $300
AMD Radeon RX 7600
~$250 new on Amazon

The RX 7600 is the best value new graphics card you can buy in 2026 for 1080p gaming. It handles every modern title at high-to-ultra settings at 60+fps, runs cool and quiet, and doesn’t require a monster PSU (130W TDP). AMD’s FSR 3 upscaling works across a wide range of games to push frames even further without quality loss.

The 8GB VRAM is the main caveat — a few newer titles push close to that limit at ultra settings. For 1080p gaming today, it’s not a real issue. For 1440p, you’ll want more VRAM.

+ Pros
  • Best FPS-per-dollar new
  • Low power draw (130W)
  • FSR 3 upscaling support
  • Runs cool and quiet
− Cons
  • 8GB VRAM limit
  • No ray tracing performance
  • 1440p only at medium settings

🥇 Best for DLSS / Ray Tracing
NVIDIA GeForce RTX 4060
~$300 new on Amazon

The RTX 4060 costs $50 more than the RX 7600 but brings NVIDIA’s DLSS 3 Frame Generation — which can nearly double frame rates in supported titles. If you play games that support DLSS (most major releases do), the RTX 4060 effectively punches above its price tier. Ray tracing performance is also far ahead of AMD at this price.

Raw rasterization performance is close to the RX 7600. The premium is worth paying if DLSS 3 and NVIDIA features matter to you. Not worth it if you only play older or non-DLSS titles.

+ Pros
  • DLSS 3 Frame Generation
  • Better ray tracing
  • NVIDIA Broadcast, ShadowPlay
  • Efficient (115W TDP)
− Cons
  • 8GB VRAM same as RX 7600
  • $50 more for similar raw perf
  • DLSS only helps in supported games

🥉 Best Used Pick
AMD Radeon RX 6700 XT (Used)
~$180–200 used

If you’re willing to buy used, the RX 6700 XT is a steal. It has 12GB VRAM — more than the new $300 cards — and delivers strong 1080p and even solid 1440p gaming performance. The higher VRAM is particularly valuable in newer open-world titles that aggressively load high-res textures.

Check eBay, Facebook Marketplace, or local listings. Look for cards sold by gamers (not miners) and ask for a video of it running a game before buying.

+ Pros
  • 12GB VRAM at sub-$200
  • Strong 1080p–1440p performance
  • Great value vs new cards
− Cons
  • No warranty on used
  • Higher power draw (230W)
  • Unknown wear history

VRAM: Why It Matters More in 2026

VRAM has become a talking point in 2026 because several AAA titles now recommend 10–12GB at ultra settings. At 1080p high settings, 8GB is still fine for most games. But if you plan to keep a GPU for 3+ years, more VRAM future-proofs you. It’s one reason the used RX 6700 XT (12GB) is so compelling over a new 8GB card at a similar price.

💡 AMD vs NVIDIA at Budget Tier: Which to Choose? For pure 1080p gaming without upscaling: RX 7600 wins on price. For DLSS 3 games or content creation: RTX 4060 wins. For used buyers who want longevity: RX 6700 XT wins on VRAM. There’s no universally "best" choice — it depends on your games and workflow.

What to Avoid

  • GTX 10 series (1060, 1070): Too old — driver support fading, no DX12 Ultimate, not worth money in 2026.
  • RTX 4060 Ti: Only ~15% faster than RTX 4060 for ~40% more money. Terrible value at budget tier.
  • Any GPU with less than 8GB VRAM in 2026: 4GB and 6GB cards are already struggling in newer titles.
  • No-name brands on Amazon: Stick to ASUS, MSI, Sapphire, PowerColor, Gigabyte, EVGA for GPU brands.

The Verdict

For most budget PC builders in 2026, the RX 7600 at ~$250 is the sweet spot — new card, warranty, great 1080p performance, low power draw. Step up to the RTX 4060 only if you actively play DLSS 3 supported titles. And if your budget is tight, a used RX 6700 XT under $200 offers more VRAM than either new option.

Pair whichever GPU you choose with a matching CPU — a Ryzen 5 7600 or i5-13400F won’t bottleneck any of these cards at 1080p.
